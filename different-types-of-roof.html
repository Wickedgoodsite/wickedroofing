Different types of roof

Overview of the most common roofing materials

However, in general, here are the models we see most often: Flat roof; Pitched roof; Mansard roof. Then there are subcategories for each structure, especially for pitched roofs. This type of roofing can have one pitch (single slope), two pitches or several pitches. It is one of the most common types of roofing, especially in small towns or suburbs. In big cities, we see a lot of flat roofs with covering materials such as TPO, EPDM or multilayer membranes. To choose the roofing model that will best suit your home, you will need to take into account several factors including the size of the house, weather conditions, budget and aesthetic considerations.

This is a very important factor when carrying out a roof renovation project! It’s hard to present an order of magnitude of the prices associated with roof renovations, as there are many factors to take into account. Indeed, a simple repair will not create the same expenses as building a new roof.





Metal roof

Are you looking for a highly resistant material to make sure that your roof has a long lifespan? Sheet metal roofing may be an interesting option. You will find some of the strongest materials available on the market. In general, metal roofing is also highly waterproof, which is an interesting feature in a climate like ours. Finally, it should be noted that metal roofs can be very energy efficient. Regarding the disadvantages, it must be taken into account that certain environments can be somewhat hostile to metal, especially in places where there may be accumulations of salt that will degrade surfaces. Lastly, sheet metal roofs can also be more difficult to install.





Slate roof

Slatestone has been used as a roofing material for ages and has experienced a revival since the 1970s. Considered a noble and elegant product, slate is also used for floors, counters and walls. If you want to fully enjoy the benefits of slate, you will opt for a natural slate. However, when you have to deal with a smaller budget, there is also synthetic slate that has a shorter lifespan.





Asphalt shingle roof

Asphalt shingles are widely used on roofs in Canada. This is an affordable material with a bit of a bad reputation, but it offers very interesting advantages. For satisfactory results, it is important to choose the right type of shingle and hire experts who will be able to ensure that the material is installed properly.





Flat roof

Do you have a flat roof? There are 4 main types of materials designed for flat roofs. Among these are the TPO and EPDM coatings, which are monolayer-type membranes. The two materials have in common the fact that they are waterproof, resistant and durable. With proper maintenance, they can have a lifespan between 15 and 35 years. However, be aware that TPO and EPDM are quite different materials and they each have their pros and cons.
